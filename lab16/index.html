Lab 16: Fancy CSS 2

In this lab 16 I'm learing more CSS codes like how to make an image be a background, different effects for elements using transitions, making animations for elements, and making columns for text.

Challenges

This lab was a bit hard to do but the only easy things were how to make the text into columns and how to make the image as a background. The transition was difficult to do since I didn't know what kinds of things I could do so I looked for examples to give me some ideas. I saw the rotating transition and thought about making a star rotate by adjusting the rotating degrees to be more while it was hovered over it. The animation wasn't too hard I created a div for it so that it will only move inside of it. And using the w3schools I used the example of a square going around clockwise and copied the codes and adjusted the animation keyframes for the left and top and retested over and over until it was perfect. Other website examples were great for reference and jsfiddle perfect for test them.

Problems

The one I had the most problem was the transtion to make the text slowy go down without it to just appear at it's location because I wanted it to slowly move down. I was struggling until I figure it out when I was looking on the internet for examples I saw one kind of what I wanted but it was going horizontal instead of vertical. I copied the codes and changed the (5,0) to (0,5) to only make it move verical and adjusted some of how much I wanted it to go down, and the transition speed. After saving and loading the page again it was working great and adjust more of the height to move more down to be hidden.

Results

As you can see in my Lab 16 when you go to the section where there is a pixel background. It has all of my learned skills I used transition to make the star at the top right corner spin 1800 degrees when you hover over it and when you are not hovering over it does a backward 1800 degree to be in it's orginal position. I made the text be into 3 columns and I added a transition transform: translate(0, 43em) going at a linear speed while it slowly floated down and when it reaches the bottom of the pixel background the text is hidden like in a videogame screen or a movie and quickly goes back up. Finally the animation I added was a kirby images that goes clockwise around the pixel background infinite. I really enjoyed doing this lab learning especially transitions and animations and it's complicated but fun.
