Graffiti Against The System: GATS - Oakland Icon

Graffiti Against The System (GATS) is an Oakland based graffiti artist known for the face/mask character he paints around the bay and his conscious approach to graffiti art. He does not claim to be a savant or an expert in the subject, he is well known because his work speaks for itself and is an iconic figure in the Bay Area graffiti scene because of his ability to get his pieces up and stay up. He began writing his moniker GATS in Los Angeles 2005 but since then has had multiple contemporary gallery productions and shows. He has been interviewed by established magazines and had a small documentary series back in 2012 depicting his work writing a powerful poem across the city of Oakland following the Oscar Grant shooting. The mask character he draws symbolizes a few things, its first an anti-establishment statement. In his own words taken from the docu series in 2012, “It represents double identity...I associate masks with the freedom of speech. Because it is the only time you can speak freely without being persecuted, and so I will never take mine off ”. The tears drawn on the characters represent the lost lives of loved ones, people wronged by the system and wisdom. The teeth, beard or tentacles coming down from the face symbolize experience and wisdom gained over time. His bold thick lines and incredibly detailed work is iconic and something to admire. GATS will continue to be a presence in the Bay Area and as his work continues to find more and more professional recognition, he is slowly becoming a graffiti artist worthy of an all time great title.

-Nick Rodarte
